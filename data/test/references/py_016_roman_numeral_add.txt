This function takes two Roman numeral strings `a` and `b` and returns their sum as a Roman numeral string. It works by converting each input to an integer via a right-to-left scan that subtracts any symbol whose value is less than the previously seen one (handling subtractive notation like IV or IX), summing the two integers, then greedily converting the result back using a descending table of value/symbol pairs including the subtractive forms. Inputs are uppercased before parsing, but no validation is performed, so malformed numerals or characters outside `IVXLCDM` will raise a KeyError, and since `to_roman` has no upper bound, very large sums simply produce long runs of `M`.
