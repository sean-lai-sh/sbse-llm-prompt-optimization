Computes the weighted Levenshtein edit distance between `source` and `target` using a classical bottom-up dynamic programming approach with an (m+1)×(n+1) table, where insertion, deletion, and replacement costs are configurable (defaulting to 1). The first row and column are seeded with cumulative insert/delete costs to represent transforming to/from the empty string, and each cell takes the minimum of the three operation costs, or inherits the diagonal value when characters match. Returns the minimum total cost as a number; runs in O(m·n) time and space.
