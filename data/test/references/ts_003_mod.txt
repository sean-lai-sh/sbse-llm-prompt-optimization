Computes the mathematical modulo of `a` by `b`, returning a non-negative result when `b` is positive (unlike JavaScript's native `%` operator, which preserves the sign of the dividend). Takes two numbers as input and returns their modulo as a number, throwing an `Error` if `b` is zero. The `((a % b) + b) % b` idiom normalizes negative remainders into the canonical `[0, b)` range.
