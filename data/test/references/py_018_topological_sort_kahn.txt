This function implements Kahn's algorithm to compute a topological ordering of a directed graph represented as an adjacency-list dict mapping nodes to their successor lists, returning the ordered list of nodes or None if the graph contains a cycle. It first computes in-degrees for all nodes (including successors not present as keys in the input), then repeatedly dequeues zero-in-degree nodes and decrements the in-degrees of their successors. Nodes with initial in-degree zero are seeded into the queue in sorted order to make the output deterministic for ties, and a cycle is detected when the final order's length doesn't match the total node count.
