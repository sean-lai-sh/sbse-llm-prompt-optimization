Computes the Levenshtein (edit) distance between two strings `a` and `b`, returning the minimum number of single-character insertions, deletions, or substitutions needed to transform one into the other. It uses the standard iterative two-row dynamic programming approach, maintaining only the previous and current rows of the DP matrix to achieve O(len(a)·len(b)) time and O(len(b)) space. Fast-path checks short-circuit when the strings are equal or either is empty, returning 0 or the length of the non-empty string respectively.
