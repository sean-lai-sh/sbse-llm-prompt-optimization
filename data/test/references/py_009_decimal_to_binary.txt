The function converts an integer `n` into its binary string representation, returning "0" for zero and prepending a minus sign for negative inputs (sign-magnitude form rather than two's complement). It works by repeatedly extracting the least significant bit via `n & 1` and right-shifting until `n` becomes zero, then reversing and joining the collected bits. The output is a string with no "0b" prefix, and negative numbers are handled by taking the absolute value before the bit-extraction loop.
