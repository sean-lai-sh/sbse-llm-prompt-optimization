The `negate` function is a simple unary numeric utility that takes a single argument `x` and returns its arithmetic negation by applying JavaScript's unary minus operator. For numeric inputs it returns the additive inverse (e.g., `5` becomes `-5`, `-3` becomes `3`), and due to IEEE 754 semantics it converts `0` to `-0` and vice versa, while `NaN` remains `NaN`. Non-numeric inputs are first coerced to a number via the unary minus operator, so values like strings or `null` may yield `NaN` or coerced numeric results (e.g., `undefined` produces `NaN`).
