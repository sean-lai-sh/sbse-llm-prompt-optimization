Returns an array where each position holds the product of all input elements except the one at that index, computed without division. It performs two linear passes: a left-to-right sweep that fills result[i] with the running product of everything to the left, then a right-to-left sweep that multiplies result[i] by the running product of everything to the right. Runs in O(n) time and O(1) auxiliary space beyond the output array, and produces 1 at any index where the input is empty on one side; behavior with zeros is correct because no division is used.
