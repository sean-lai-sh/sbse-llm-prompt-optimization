Performs recursive depth-first search starting at a given vertex on a Map-based adjacency-list graph, returning the visit order plus per-node discovery time, finish time, and DFS-tree parent (with roots mapped to null). A monotonically increasing timer is incremented when each node is first visited and again when its recursion finishes, mirroring the textbook DFS timestamping. After the start-rooted traversal it iterates remaining graph keys and re-roots DFS on any still-unseen node, so disconnected components are also covered; total work is O(V + E) and recursion depth can blow the call stack on long paths.
