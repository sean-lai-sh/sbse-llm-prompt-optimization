Implements a generic binary-heap-backed priority queue parameterized by a comparator (treated as a min-heap when the comparator returns negative for a "smaller" element). It exposes push, pop, peek, size, and isEmpty closures over an array-based heap, with bubbleUp comparing each new element against its parent at index floor((i - 1) / 2) and bubbleDown selecting the smaller of the two children at indices 2i+1 and 2i+2. push and pop run in O(log n), while peek, size, and isEmpty are O(1); pop returns undefined on an empty heap and uses the standard swap-last-with-root-then-sift-down pattern.
