Returns the list of all prime numbers up to and including limit using the Sieve of Eratosthenes. It allocates a boolean array of size limit+1, marks 0 and 1 as non-prime, and for each i from 2 to floor(sqrt(limit)) marks every multiple starting at i*i as composite; it then collects the indices that remain True. Time complexity is O(n log log n) and space is O(n); inputs less than 2 are short-circuited to return an empty list.
