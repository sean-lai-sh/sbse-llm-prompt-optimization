Recursively generates every permutation of the characters in string s, returning them as a list of strings. For each index it removes the chosen character, recurses on the remaining substring, and prepends that character to each permutation returned, with a base case that returns [s] when the input has length 0 or 1. Time complexity is O(n * n!) and space is O(n * n!) for the result, and duplicate characters yield duplicate permutations because the algorithm does not deduplicate.
