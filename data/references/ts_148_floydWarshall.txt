Computes all-pairs shortest paths over a weight matrix using the classic Floyd-Warshall triple loop with intermediate vertex k as the outermost, returning the resulting distance matrix, a next-hop matrix for path reconstruction, and a hasNegativeCycle flag. It initializes diagonals to 0 and missing edges to Infinity, then for each (i, j) it relaxes via dist[i][k] + dist[k][j] when both legs are finite, updating next[i][j] to next[i][k] when an improvement is found. After relaxation it detects negative cycles by checking whether any dist[i][i] became negative, and the overall algorithm runs in O(n^3) time and O(n^2) space.
