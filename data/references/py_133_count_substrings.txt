Counts overlapping occurrences of substring sub inside string s by repeatedly calling str.find starting from one position past the previous match, incrementing a counter for each successful find until find returns -1. By advancing start by 1 rather than len(sub), the function reports overlapping matches, distinguishing it from str.count which counts only non-overlapping ones. It returns 0 immediately for an empty sub to avoid an infinite loop, and overall runs in O(n * m) worst-case time where n and m are the lengths of s and sub.
