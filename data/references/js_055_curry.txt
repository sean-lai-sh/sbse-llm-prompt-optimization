Transforms a function fn into a curried form whose arity is inferred from fn.length, returning a wrapper that either invokes fn when enough arguments have accumulated or returns another collector function awaiting the rest. Each partial application concatenates new arguments onto the previously gathered ones via args.concat(moreArgs) and recurses through the same curried closure, so calls may be split arbitrarily (e.g. f(1)(2,3) or f(1,2)(3)). this is preserved through fn.apply on the final call. It does not handle variadic functions reliably because fn.length only counts non-rest, non-defaulted parameters, and extra trailing arguments beyond arity are passed through to fn.
