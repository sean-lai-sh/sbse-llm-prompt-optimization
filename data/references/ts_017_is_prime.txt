Decides whether an integer is prime by rejecting values below 2, special-casing 2, eliminating even numbers, and then trial-dividing by odd candidates up to sqrt(n). It takes a number and returns a boolean in O(sqrt(n)) time and O(1) space. The loop bound i * i <= n avoids a square-root call, and the function makes no special accommodation for non-integer or negative inputs beyond the n < 2 short circuit.
