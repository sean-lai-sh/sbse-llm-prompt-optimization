Rounds the number n to the given number of decimal places by scaling by 10^decimals, applying Math.round (round-half-away-from-zero for positives, half-toward-zero for negatives in JavaScript), and dividing back. Runs in O(1) time and accepts negative or zero values for decimals to round to tens, hundreds, etc. Because the multiplication and division use binary floating-point, results can still exhibit small representation errors (for example 1.005 may not round as a human would expect), and very large decimals values can lose precision or overflow.
