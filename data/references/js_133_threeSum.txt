Finds all unique triplets in nums whose sum is zero using the sort-and-two-pointer technique. It first sorts nums ascending in place, then for each anchor index i it sweeps two pointers (left = i+1, right = end) inward, advancing left when the sum is too small and right when too large, and recording the triplet on a hit. Duplicates are skipped both at the anchor (by comparing with nums[i-1]) and after recording a hit (by skipping equal neighbors at left and right) to ensure the output contains no repeated triplets. Time complexity is O(n^2) after the O(n log n) sort; the input array is mutated by the sort.
