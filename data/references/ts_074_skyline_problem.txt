Computes the skyline outline of a set of buildings as a list of [x, height] critical points using a divide-and-conquer approach in O(n log n) time. The recursive solve splits the building list in half and merges the two child skylines via a two-pointer sweep that, at each x event, updates the active heights h1 and h2 from the left and right skylines and emits a point whenever the maximum of those heights changes. Returns an empty array when there are no buildings; each base case emits two points [left, height] and [right, 0] for a single building, and consecutive equal-height points are suppressed in the merge to keep the output minimal.
