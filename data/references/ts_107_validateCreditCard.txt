Validates a credit card number string using the Luhn (mod-10) checksum algorithm. It first strips all non-digit characters, rejects lengths outside the 13-19 range, then iterates from the rightmost digit, doubling every second digit and subtracting 9 if the doubled value exceeds 9, and finally returns true when the running sum is divisible by 10. Runs in O(n) time over the digit count and validates only the checksum, not the issuer's BIN range or expiration.
