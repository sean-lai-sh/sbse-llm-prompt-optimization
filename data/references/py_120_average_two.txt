Returns the arithmetic mean of two numeric inputs a and b by computing (a + b) / 2 with true division, so the result is always a float. Runs in O(1) time and is susceptible to intermediate overflow only for non-Python numeric types since Python ints are unbounded. The implementation can lose precision for very large floats due to the addition step before division, unlike the more numerically stable a/2 + b/2 form.
