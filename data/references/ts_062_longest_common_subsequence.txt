Computes the longest common subsequence of strings a and b via a bottom-up (m+1) by (n+1) DP table where dp[i][j] holds the LCS length of the prefixes a[0..i-1] and b[0..j-1]. After filling the table in O(m*n) time and space using the classic equal/max recurrence, it back-tracks from dp[m][n], moving diagonally on character matches and otherwise toward the larger neighbor (preferring up on ties), collecting characters in reverse and joining them. Returns an LCSResult with both length and one valid subsequence; empty inputs simply yield {length: 0, subsequence: ""}.
