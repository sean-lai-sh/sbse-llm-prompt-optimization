Determines whether a string is a palindrome after lowercasing it and stripping every character that is not an ASCII letter or digit. It accepts a string and returns a boolean, comparing the cleaned string to its reversed form in O(n) time and O(n) space. The case-insensitive, non-alphanumeric-stripping behavior makes phrases like "A man, a plan, a canal: Panama" qualify, while non-ASCII letters are discarded rather than normalized.
