Recursively flattens a nested record into a single-level object whose keys are dot-joined paths from the root, accepting an optional prefix used during recursion. For each entry, plain non-null, non-array object values are recursed with the extended prefix and merged via Object.assign, while primitives, arrays, and null are written directly under the composed key. Arrays are treated as leaf values rather than being indexed, and keys that already contain "." will collide with genuine nesting on a later unflatten.
