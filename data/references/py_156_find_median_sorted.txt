Computes the median of two sorted integer lists in O(log(min(m, n))) time by binary-searching a partition of the shorter array such that every element on the left side is no greater than every element on the right. It swaps the inputs to ensure nums1 is the shorter, then narrows the partition index until the cross-conditions max_left1 <= min_right2 and max_left2 <= min_right1 hold, returning either the maximum of the lefts or the average of max-left and min-right depending on the parity of m+n. Sentinels of negative and positive infinity handle empty partition halves; the function returns 0.0 only as an unreachable fallback when the loop terminates without finding a valid split.
