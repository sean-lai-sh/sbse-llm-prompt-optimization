Computes the median of the union of two sorted arrays nums1 and nums2 in O(log(min(m, n))) time without merging them, by binary-searching a partition i in the smaller array and deriving the matching partition j = halfLen - i in the larger array. At each step it shrinks the search range based on the cross-comparison nums2[j-1] > nums1[i] or nums1[i-1] > nums2[j], converging on the partition where every left-side element is less than or equal to every right-side element. For odd combined lengths it returns the maximum of the two left sides; for even lengths it averages that with the minimum of the two right sides. It first swaps arguments to keep nums1 the smaller array, and throws an Error if the inputs are not sorted (loop falls through).
