Computes the arithmetic mean of the numeric array nums by reducing it to a sum and dividing by the length, running in O(n) time and O(1) extra space. To avoid a division by zero the function short-circuits to 0 on an empty array, conflating "no data" with a true zero mean. Non-numeric or NaN entries propagate through the addition and yield NaN, and very large arrays can lose precision due to repeated double-precision additions.
