Applies a Caesar cipher to a text string by shifting each alphabetic character by the given integer shift, returning the encoded string. For each character it picks the appropriate base ('A' or 'a') to preserve case, computes (ord(ch) - base + shift) mod 26 and adds the base back; non-alphabetic characters are passed through unchanged. The function runs in O(n) time and space, handles negative shifts and shifts greater than 26 correctly via the modulo, and only supports ASCII letters (Unicode letters outside A-Z/a-z are left unmodified).
