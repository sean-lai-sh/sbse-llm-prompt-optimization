Implements the classic bubble sort algorithm, taking a list of integers and returning the same list sorted in ascending order. It uses two nested loops to repeatedly compare adjacent elements and swap them when out of order, with the inner loop bound shrinking by one each pass since the largest unsorted element bubbles to its final position. The sort runs in O(n^2) time and O(1) extra space, mutates the input list in place, and lacks an early-exit optimization so it always performs the full n passes even on already-sorted input.
