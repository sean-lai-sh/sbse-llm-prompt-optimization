Computes the least common multiple of two integers via the identity lcm(a, b) = |a| / gcd(a, b) * |b|, where the GCD is computed inline with the Euclidean algorithm. It takes two numbers and returns a number in O(log(min(a, b))) time and O(1) space. It explicitly returns 0 when either operand is zero to avoid division by zero, and dividing before multiplying reduces the chance of intermediate overflow.
