Recursively builds a deep copy of a dictionary that may contain nested dicts and lists, returning a brand-new structure that shares no mutable containers with the input. Nested dict values are copied via recursive calls, list values are copied with a list comprehension that recurses on dict elements, and all other values are copied by reference. The function only handles dicts and lists explicitly (not tuples, sets, or custom objects), and recursion depth is bounded by the nesting depth of the input.
