Tests whether a positive integer n is an exact power of two using the classic bit trick (n & (n - 1)) === 0 combined with a positivity guard, returning a boolean in O(1) time and space. The trick works because powers of two have exactly one set bit in their binary representation, so subtracting one flips that bit and clears the lower bits, making the AND zero. The n > 0 check rejects 0 (which would falsely satisfy the AND condition) and all negative numbers. Because JavaScript's bitwise operators coerce operands to 32-bit signed integers, values above 2^31 - 1 or non-integer numbers will produce incorrect results.
