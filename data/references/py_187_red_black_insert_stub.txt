Inserts each value from the input list into a left-leaning red-black tree and returns the in-order traversal of the final tree as a sorted list of unique values. Each insertion uses standard BST recursion followed by three rebalancing steps on the way back up: rotate left when only the right child is red, rotate right when there are two consecutive left red links, and flip colors when both children are red; the root is recolored black after each top-level insert. Duplicate inserts are no-ops, the tree stays balanced with height O(log n), and total cost is O(n log n) time and O(n) space.
