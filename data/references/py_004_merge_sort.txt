Recursively sorts a list of integers using top-down merge sort, returning a new sorted list rather than mutating the input. It splits the list at the midpoint, recursively sorts each half, then merges the two sorted halves by repeatedly appending the smaller front element and finally extending with whatever remains. The algorithm runs in O(n log n) time and O(n) auxiliary space, is stable thanks to the <= comparison, and handles the empty and single-element base cases by returning the input unchanged.
