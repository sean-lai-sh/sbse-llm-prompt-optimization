Converts a Roman numeral string to its integer value by scanning the uppercased characters from right to left and tracking the previous digit's value. When the current numeral's value is less than the previous one it is subtracted, otherwise it is added, which correctly handles subtractive pairs like IV, IX, XL, etc. The function runs in O(n) time and O(1) space; it raises KeyError on characters outside I, V, X, L, C, D, M and does not validate well-formedness of the numeral.
