Recursively merges two dicts where override's values take precedence over base, but when both sides hold dicts at the same key the function recurses to merge them rather than replacing wholesale. The top level is shallow-copied with base.copy() so the original base is not mutated, but nested dicts inside base may be shared with the result and mutated by the recursion. Runs in O(n) time relative to total keys; lists and other non-dict values are not merged element-wise and are simply overwritten.
