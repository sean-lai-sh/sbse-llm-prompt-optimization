Sorts a copy of an array by an ordered list of {key, direction} criteria, breaking ties by falling through to the next criterion. It returns a new array (or [] for non-array input, or a shallow copy for empty criteria) and applies per-criterion direction with "asc" as the default. Within each criterion, null and undefined are sorted last regardless of direction, and the < operator decides ordering for non-equal defined values. Time complexity is the sort's O(n log n) multiplied by O(k) criteria checks per comparison.
