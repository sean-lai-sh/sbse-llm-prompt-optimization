Removes duplicate characters from string s while preserving the order of first occurrence by iterating once and tracking already-seen characters in a set. Each new character is appended to a list accumulator, which is joined into a single string at the end to avoid quadratic concatenation cost. Runs in O(n) average time and O(k) space where k is the number of distinct characters, and the comparison is case-sensitive so 'A' and 'a' are treated as distinct.
