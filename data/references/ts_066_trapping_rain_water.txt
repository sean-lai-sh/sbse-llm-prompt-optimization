Computes the total units of water trapped over an elevation map height using the two-pointer technique in O(n) time and O(1) extra space. It maintains left and right pointers along with running leftMax and rightMax, always advancing the side whose current bar is shorter; if that bar is below its side's running max, the difference is added to the accumulated water, otherwise the running max is updated. Inputs of length less than 3 short-circuit to 0, and non-negative heights are assumed; negative values would produce nonsensical results.
