Validates a credit card number string using length checks and the Luhn checksum algorithm. It first rejects non-strings, strips spaces and hyphens, and requires the remainder to be 13 to 19 ASCII digits, then walks the digits right-to-left doubling every second digit and subtracting 9 when the doubled value exceeds 9. The function returns true when the resulting sum is divisible by 10 and false otherwise. It performs no issuer-specific (Visa/Mastercard/etc.) prefix checks and runs in O(n) time over the digit count.
