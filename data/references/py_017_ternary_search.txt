Performs iterative ternary search on a sorted list of integers, returning the matching index of target or None. Each iteration computes two split points mid1 and mid2 that divide the current window into three roughly equal parts and either returns immediately on a hit or restricts the search to the left third, right third, or middle third based on comparisons. Time complexity is O(log3 n) with O(1) auxiliary space; despite making fewer recursive splits than binary search, it performs more comparisons per step and requires the array to be sorted in ascending order.
