Returns a new list containing the unique elements of lst while preserving their first-occurrence order, implemented by constructing a dict via dict.fromkeys and converting its keys back to a list. This relies on the insertion-order guarantee of Python 3.7+ dicts and runs in O(n) average time and space, with deduplication driven by element hashability and equality. Unhashable elements such as nested lists raise TypeError, since dict keys must be hashable.
