Returns a new string in which every whitespace-delimited word has its first character uppercased and the rest lowercased, using str.capitalize on each token. The text is split on any whitespace and the resulting words are joined back with single spaces, normalizing runs of whitespace in the process. Runtime is O(n) in the input length; unlike str.title it works on whitespace boundaries only, so apostrophes and hyphens inside a word do not introduce additional capitalization.
