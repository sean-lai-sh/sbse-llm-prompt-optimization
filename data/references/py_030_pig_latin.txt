Converts a single English word to Pig Latin and returns the transformed string. If the word starts with a vowel (a, e, i, o, u in either case) it appends 'way'; otherwise it scans forward to the first vowel, then returns the substring from that vowel to the end concatenated with the leading consonant cluster and the suffix 'ay'. Time and space are O(n), and edge cases like an empty string or a word containing no vowels are not explicitly handled (an empty string raises IndexError on word[0], and a vowel-less word is returned as itself plus 'ay').
