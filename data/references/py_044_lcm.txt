Computes the least common multiple of two integers a and b and returns abs(a * b) // gcd(a, b). It defines a nested iterative Euclidean gcd helper and short-circuits to return 0 if either input is zero, since the LCM with zero is conventionally defined as zero. Time complexity is dominated by the gcd at O(log min(|a|, |b|)), space is O(1), and the abs call ensures a non-negative result for negative inputs.
