Returns the maximum sum that can be obtained from a list of non-negative house values such that no two chosen houses are adjacent, solving the classic house-robber DP. It iterates once, maintaining two rolling scalars prev2 and prev1 representing the best totals ending two and one steps back and updating them with prev1 = max(prev1, prev2 + n), giving O(n) time and O(1) space. The function explicitly returns 0 for an empty list and works for any list of integers, including negatives where it would still pick the best non-adjacent subset (potentially the empty subset, value 0).
