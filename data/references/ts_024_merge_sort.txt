Sorts a numeric array in ascending order via top-down merge sort, recursively splitting the array at its midpoint and combining the sorted halves with a helper merge that walks two pointers using the <= comparison. It takes a number[] and returns a number[] in O(n log n) time and O(n log n) space due to the slice-based recursion. The merge's <= choice preserves the relative order of equal elements, making the sort stable; arrays of length 0 or 1 are returned as-is without copying.
