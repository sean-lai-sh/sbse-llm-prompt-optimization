Rounds a number n to the specified number of decimal places by scaling by 10^decimals via Math.pow, applying Math.round (which uses round-half-up to positive infinity), then dividing back, all in O(1) time and space. Negative decimals values round to powers of ten (e.g. roundTo(1234, -2) yields 1200). The approach is subject to floating-point representation errors for values that cannot be exactly represented in binary (e.g. roundTo(1.005, 2) yields 1, not 1.01) and uses banker-incompatible round-half-up for ties on positive numbers. NaN, Infinity, or non-numeric inputs propagate through Math.round to produce NaN or Infinity.
