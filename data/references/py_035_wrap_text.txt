Wraps a text string into a list of lines no longer than width characters, splitting on whitespace and packing as many words as possible per line. It iterates over the whitespace-split tokens, accumulating words into current_line as long as adding the next word (plus a separating space) keeps the running length within width; otherwise it flushes the current line and starts a new one with that word. Time complexity is O(n) in the number of words; words longer than width are placed on their own line but are not broken apart, and the length-tracking arithmetic for the separator space is slightly inconsistent (it under-counts the separator on the second word added).
