Recursively determines whether a string is a palindrome after lowercasing and stripping all non-alphanumeric characters, returning True or False. It compares the first and last characters of the cleaned string and recurses on the inner substring, returning True when length collapses to 0 or 1. Note that the cleaning step is repeated on each recursive call (since slicing produces an already-cleaned substring this is harmless but wastes work) and the slicing creates O(n) new strings, giving O(n^2) total time and O(n) recursion depth.
