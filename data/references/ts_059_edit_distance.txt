Computes the Levenshtein edit distance between strings a and b via bottom-up dynamic programming, with the standard recurrence over insertion, deletion, and substitution. It first swaps the inputs so that b is the shorter string and then keeps only two rolling rows prev and curr of length n+1, giving O(m*n) time and O(min(m,n)) space. Row prev is initialized to 0..n representing the cost of inserting the prefix of b into an empty string, and the two rows are swapped after each outer iteration so the final answer is prev[n].
