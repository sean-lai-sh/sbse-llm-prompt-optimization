Computes the total units of rainwater trapped between bars in the height array using the two-pointer technique in O(n) time and O(1) extra space. Two pointers (left, right) converge inward and track running leftMax and rightMax; the smaller side advances and contributes leftMax - height[left] (or symmetric on the right) of water whenever the current bar is below the side's running maximum, otherwise the running maximum is updated. It returns 0 immediately for null/undefined input or arrays of fewer than 3 bars since no water can be held. The algorithm assumes non-negative integer heights and does not allocate any auxiliary arrays.
