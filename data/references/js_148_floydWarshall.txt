Computes all-pairs shortest paths on a directed weighted graph of n nodes via the Floyd-Warshall dynamic-programming triple loop in O(n^3) time and O(n^2) space. It returns {dist, next, getPath, hasNegativeCycle, getTransitiveClosure}, where dist is the distance matrix, next supports path reconstruction in O(path length), and the closure helper reports reachability based on finite distances. Negative cycles are detected by checking whether any dist[i][i] became negative after relaxation, and getPath defends against following a cycle by bounding its loop to n steps and returning null on overflow. Multiple parallel edges between the same pair are handled because direct-edge initialization keeps the minimum weight.
