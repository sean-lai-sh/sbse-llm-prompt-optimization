Recursively flattens an arbitrarily nested array into a single-level array by walking each element and recursing whenever Array.isArray returns true. It is generic over T, taking an unknown[] and returning a T[] in O(n) time and O(n) output space plus O(d) recursion depth equal to the maximum nesting level. The cast of leaf items to T is unchecked, and very deeply nested inputs could exceed the JavaScript engine's call-stack limit.
