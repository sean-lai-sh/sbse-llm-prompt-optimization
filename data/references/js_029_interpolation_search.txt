Searches for target in a sorted array arr using interpolation search, which estimates the probable position with the linear interpolation formula low + ((target - arr[low]) * (high - low)) / (arr[high] - arr[low]) and narrows the [low, high] window accordingly. Returns the index of a match or -1 when target falls outside [arr[low], arr[high]] or the window collapses without a hit. Achieves O(log log n) time on uniformly distributed sorted data but degrades to O(n) on skewed distributions, and risks division by zero when arr[low] equals arr[high] (handled by the explicit low === high branch).
