Finds the median of two sorted arrays in O(log(min(m, n))) time by binary-searching the partition position i in the shorter array and deriving the partition j = halfLen - i in the longer one. It recurses once if needed to ensure nums1 is the shorter array, then uses sentinel +/-Infinity at partition boundaries to compare maxLeft1, maxLeft2, minRight1, minRight2; the correct partition satisfies maxLeft1 <= minRight2 and maxLeft2 <= minRight1, after which the median is either maxLeft (odd total length) or the average of maxLeft and minRight. Throws an Error if the search loop exits without finding a partition, which only happens when the inputs are not actually sorted.
