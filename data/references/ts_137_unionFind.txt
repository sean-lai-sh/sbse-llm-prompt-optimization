Builds a disjoint-set (Union-Find) structure for n elements with parent and rank arrays plus a live components counter, returning find, union, connected, and componentCount closures. find performs recursive path compression by reassigning parent[x] to the discovered root, and union joins two sets using union-by-rank (attaching the shorter tree under the taller, incrementing rank on a tie) while decrementing the component count and returning false when the inputs are already connected. Operations run in near-constant amortized time (inverse Ackermann) per call thanks to both heuristics.
